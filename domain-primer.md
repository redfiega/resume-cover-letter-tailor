## 1. The Difference Between a CV and a Resume

**CV (Curriculum Vitae):**
- Exhaustive record of all professional and academic accomplishments
- Includes publications, presentations, grants, awards, teaching, service
- Can be 5-20+ pages
- Standard in academic and research contexts

**Resume:**
- Targeted, concise document tailored to a specific job posting
- Typically 1-2 pages
- Includes only the most relevant experience, skills, and accomplishments
- Standard in industry, government, and non-academic professional contexts

**Key principle:** A resume is not a shortened CV. It is a curated argument for why
this specific person is the best fit for this specific role.

## 2. Resume Structure (Standard)

A professional resume typically includes these sections in this order:

1. **Header** — Name, contact information, LinkedIn, location (city/state only)
2. **Professional Summary** — 2-4 sentences summarizing who you are and what you offer
3. **Core Skills / Technical Skills** — Brief list of relevant skills and tools
4. **Professional Experience** — Reverse chronological; bullet points per role
5. **Education** — Degrees, institutions, graduation years
6. **Optional sections** — Certifications, publications (selected), awards, languages

### Bullet Point Best Practices
- Start every bullet with a strong action verb (Led, Developed, Designed, Managed)
- Quantify accomplishments wherever possible (e.g., "Increased enrollment by 23%")
- Focus on impact, not just duties (what changed because of your work?)
- Keep bullets to 1-2 lines; no full paragraphs

---

## 3. Cover Letter Structure (Standard)

A professional cover letter includes:

1. **Opening paragraph** — Why this role, why this organization, hook the reader
2. **Body paragraph 1** — Most relevant experience directly tied to job requirements
3. **Body paragraph 2** — Second strength or accomplishment; shows range
4. **Closing paragraph** — Enthusiasm, call to action, thank you
5. **Signature** — Professional closing, full name

### Cover Letter Best Practices
- Never summarize the resume; add new information or context
- Mirror the language and tone of the job posting
- Be specific — name the organization, the role, a specific program or initiative
- Keep to one page
- Sound like a person, not a template

---

## 4. Keyword Alignment

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scan resumes for keywords before a human reads them.

**Key principle:** Use the exact language from the job posting where it accurately
describes your experience. Do not paraphrase unnecessarily.

Examples:
- If the posting says "cross-functional collaboration," use that phrase
- If the posting says "data-driven decision making," use that phrase
- If the posting says "Python," write Python — not "programming languages"

---

## 5. Tailoring Principles

When tailoring a CV to a resume for a specific posting:

**Keep:**
- Experience directly relevant to the posted role
- Accomplishments that demonstrate required skills
- Keywords and phrases from the job posting
- Quantified results and specific impacts

**Cut:**
- Experience unrelated to the posted role
- Publications or presentations not relevant to industry context
- Excessive detail on early-career or irrelevant roles
- Academic jargon when applying to industry roles

**Reframe:**
- Academic teaching experience → curriculum design, facilitation, communication
- Research experience → data analysis, project management, problem-solving
- Service roles → leadership, collaboration, organizational impact

## 6. Tone Guidelines by Industry

| Industry | Tone |
|----------|------|
| Academia | Formal, comprehensive, achievement-focused |
| Corporate/Business | Confident, results-oriented, concise |
| Nonprofit | Mission-driven, collaborative, impact-focused |
| Technology | Direct, skills-forward, innovation-focused |
| Government | Formal, detail-oriented, service-focused |
| Education (K-12/Higher Ed admin) | Warm, student-centered, leadership-focused |

## 7. Clarifying Questions — When and Why

The Question Generator agent asks 3-5 questions when:
- The CV has experience that could be framed multiple ways
- The job posting requires specific accomplishments not clearly in the CV
- The user's preferred tone or emphasis is unclear
- There are gaps between CV content and job requirements worth addressing

Good clarifying questions:
- "Which of your teaching experiences do you most want to highlight for this role?"
- "Is there a specific project or accomplishment you want featured prominently?"
- "What tone do you prefer — formal and traditional, or modern and conversational?"
- "Are there any aspects of your background you do NOT want included?"
- "Is there anything about this role that particularly excites you to mention in the cover letter?"

## 8. Visual Structure Guidelines for Word Documents

**Resume formatting:**
- Clean, single font (Calibri, Georgia, or similar)
- Name in larger font (16-18pt), body text 10-11pt
- Section headers bold and slightly larger (12pt)
- Consistent margins (0.75-1 inch)
- Bullet points aligned and consistent
- No photos, graphics, or tables (ATS compatibility)
- White space used to aid scannability

**Cover letter formatting:**
- Same font as resume for consistency
- Standard business letter format
- Single-spaced paragraphs, double space between paragraphs
- No longer than one page
- Professional closing (Sincerely, Best regards)
